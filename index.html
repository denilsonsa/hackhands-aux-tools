FAQ

Why are "bad" tools included in this page?
Because knowing a tool is "bad" is useful information. It means someone went to the trouble of trying such tool and sharing the experience in this page. It means the next person doesn't need to waste time trying it again.
Are the questions in this FAQ made up?
Probably.
